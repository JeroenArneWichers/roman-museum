BREAKFAST, LUNCH AND DINNER

Roman meals

There were three meals in ancient Rome we had Ientaculum which was breakfast. Prandium which would be modern days lunch although it was differently and I will explain this in a minute and then Coena which would be dinner let's examine all these in details.

Ientaculum the first thing we have to say about Ientaculum is that it began with a glass of water so this is something the Romans used to do. As they would drink this glass of water they would begin eating that what was their Ientaculum made of what we have quite a lot of possibilities so let's examine all of these. First of all, one very common possibility in the times of Rome was to eat the leftovers of dinner in which case we would have bread, cheese, olives and honey. Another kind of breakfast was to dunk bread into sweetened wine so this was another way to have breakfast. And thirdly was to eat bread with oil vinegar and salt. Another typical kind of breakfast was bread and figs. Now children ate differently normally they were just a breakfast or Ientaculum with bread and milk.

Prandium

Prandium, first because we need to say couple of things about the word Prandium is where the modern Italian word pranzo comes from. Although in modern Italian pranzo is different in a way because pranzo in Italy or lunch is the main meal. But for the Romans Prandium is basically a second breakfast so to speak. It was eaten at midday it was frugal and it was normally eaten standing because you have to consider that at that time most people were working in the city that were not back home so often they would simply buy something off from vendors and and consume the frugal lunch or Prandium just before going back to work. During winter they would eat hot food and during summer the would eat fresh food.

A typical Prandium consisted of bread cold meat, fish, legumes, eggs, fruit and wine. Sometimes instead of just simply drinking wine they would drink Mulsum which was a drink made of wine mixed with honey. So the main meal for the Romans was the Coena now this meal here would normally start around 3:00 or 4:00 in the afternoon and it could go on until the next morning depending of course on the social status of the people we are talking about. In early Roman time, talking about Republican times they would they would eat just a soup of legumes, milk, cheese, fresh fruit, and dry fruit as well and lard. To consider, lard was an important component of Roman diet and as much a fat. Lard was also eaten by legionaries quite a lot but with the late Republic and imperial times bread and meat start to become more common even in the lower classes.

If they had guests they would have them over for dinner and the meal was not called Coena anymore it was called Convivium having Gustum or starters Caput Cenae which were the main courses Mensa Secunda which was the dessert.

Triclinium

In the case of patricians or Roman nobility these meals would be directed by the chef and all the guests would be wearing the Vestis Cenatoria which were lavish clothing worn after the hot baths.

So as was said before, in ancient Rome most meals would be eaten standing. But as time passes we have the invention of the Triclinium which was an either oval shaped or rectangular table and around this triclinium we have the Lecti which were basically those beds where Romans would lay down and eat typical of iconography.

Now when you see an image of triclinium and leg Lecti with also women then we are talking about Imperial times because it was an imperial times that women would lay down as well and you would have these very long Cenae with music and dances and singing. initially the Cenae would be consumed in the atrium so we're talking about kind of a lobby outside the house but as houses becomes bigger we start seeing the development and building of several dining halls. The typical number of commensals was nine and multiple of nine until thirty-six young children will be sitting on stools rather than actual beds where as slaves will be sitting on the Floor.
